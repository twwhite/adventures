On Arrival

– We are covering the park entrance fee for all of our attendees, just let the ranger at the entrance station know you’re with us and they’ll waive you through – When the ranger lets you through, take your first left to get to the parking lot for the Emel House. If parking is full, there are more spots available in the main parking lot a few hundred yards from the venue. – We are asking all guests to please carpool if possible. It’s a very small state park with limited spaces. Some folks are staying at rental properties or hotels near the venue, so check in with your friends and family!

Lodging

On-Site Tent Camping

We booked a Luxury (tents), Rustic (outside), and Complimentary (includes bathrooms) group campsite experience for our VIP guests (everyone!).

The campsite is an easy, short walk from the venue.

We encourage anyone who likes to camp to join in on the fun. Susie and Tim will be camping both Thurs. and Fri. nights. It’s BYO-everything!

The group campsite accomodates 40 guests. We’ll handle this as first come (RSVP) first serve. Same goes for the 10 available overnight vehicle parking spots at the group site. Carpooling will be super helpful here!

No RVs are allowed at the groupcamp (smaller sprinter-style vans are OK).

If you want your own campsite or have a larger RV, you can reserve a regular campsite at the campground through the state park website.

There is a permanent open-faced rooved shelter with exposed bed-bunks on site for those who only want to carry a sleeping bag through TSA.

If you’re reading this right now, and you’re interested in camping, reach out to us again to confirm - we’re just double checking our camping RSVPs!

For you brave souls joining us, get in touch if you have specific questions or if you forsee needing to borrow some camping supplies! :) We can help coordinate with local outfitters to get you a rental if you need, just keep in close touch!

We will be camping on-site for a few nights surrounding, but not including the night-of the wedding.
